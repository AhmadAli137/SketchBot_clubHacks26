# Programs & Kits

Packaged experiences for different audiences — families, schools, camps, competition teams, and makers.

---

## 1. SketchBot Starter Kit

**Audience:** Families with kids ages 6-12

**Duration:** Open-ended (ongoing exploration)

**What Ships:**
- Pre-assembled SketchBot (ready to draw out of the box)
- 5 printed concept cards with QR codes linking to guided lessons
- Companion app download card (iOS + Android)
- Parent guide: setup instructions, conversation starters, extension activities
- Pack of specialty paper and fine-tip markers

**Pricing Model:** One-time purchase

**Key Design Decisions:**
- Zero soldering, zero configuration — plug in USB-C, scan QR, start drawing
- Parent guide includes "questions to ask" aligned to each concept card
- Concept cards are physical (tactile, collectible) but link to digital lessons
- Additional concept card packs sold separately (expansion model)

**Duration:** Semester-long (15-18 weeks)

- Professional development webinar for teachers (1 hour)

**Pricing Model:** Annual subscription per classroom

**Curriculum Alignment:**
| Standard | SketchBot Connection |
|----------|---------------------|
| NGSS MS-ETS1 (Engineering Design) | Full design-build-test cycle through drawing challenges |

## 3. Summer Camp Pack

**Audience:** Camps, libraries, community centers

**Duration:** 1-week or 2-week programs

**What Ships:**
- 10-bot fleet with shared charging station
- Daily themed lesson plans (5 days or 10 days)
- Showcase day template (setup guide, judging rubric, certificate PDFs)

## 5. SketchBot Pro (DIY Build-Your-Own)

**Audience:** Advanced teens, makers, hackathon participants, homeschool families

**Duration:** Build project (20-40 hours assembly + firmware)

**What Ships:**
- Custom PCB (ESP32-S3 based) — bare board, student solders components
- Bill of Materials with sourcing links (all parts < $60 total)
- Step-by-step assembly manual (illustrated, 40+ pages)
- Firmware repository access (open-source, Arduino framework)
- Open-source CAD files (STEP, STL) for 3D-printed chassis
- Access to the same companion app and AI pipeline as pre-built kits

**Build Stages:**
1. **PCB Assembly** — Solder motor drivers, ESP32 module, connectors (teaches SMD soldering)
2. **Chassis Fabrication** — 3D print frame, press-fit bearings, mount motors
3. **Wiring** — Connect motors, camera mount, power supply (teaches wire management)
4. **Firmware Flash** — Clone repo, configure WiFi, flash via USB (teaches embedded dev)
5. **Calibration** — Run calibration routine, adjust PID gains, verify accuracy
6. **First Draw** — Connect to companion app, submit first prompt, celebrate

**Why This Matters:**
Students who build the machine from scratch develop a fundamentally different relationship with it than those who use a pre-built product. They understand every layer — from transistor-level motor control to cloud-based AI generation. This is the ultimate "demystification" product.
